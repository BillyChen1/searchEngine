renault boss hails great year

strong sales outside western europe helped renault boost its profits by more than 40% in 2004 although the firm warned of lower margins this year

frances second largest carmaker enjoyed a healthy 43% rise in net profits to 24bn euros 31bn; 29bn as sales rose 8% to 407bn euros the firm said strong demand outside western europe and the good performance of its megane range lifted its results chairman louis schweitzer said 2004 had been a great year for the firm

renault sold more than 24 million vehicles in 2004 an increase of 4% on the previous year growth came mainly from outside western europe with particularly strong sales in turkey russia and north africa

in total sales outside western europe  renaults core market  rose 165% japanese carmaker nissan  in which renault owns a 44% stake  contributed 17bn euros in net income over the year nissan chairman carlos ghosn is to succeed mr schweitzer at the head of renault later this year

renault said the outlook for the industry in europe this year was stable with small growth forecast in other regions the firm will benefit from the launch of a new clio model in the coming year and the rollout of the logan in many markets however the firm said it expected operating margins to be lower in 2005 at 4% of sales as opposed to 5% in a sluggish market and an environment impacted by the rise in raw material prices renault intends to continue to grow its global sales the company said in a statement
