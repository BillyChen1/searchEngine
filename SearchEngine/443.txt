kraft cuts snack ads for children

kraft plans to cut back on advertising of products like oreo cookies and sugary koolaid drinks as part of an effort to promote healthy eating

the largest us food maker will also add a label to its more nutritional and lowfat brands to promote the benefits kraft rival pepsico began a similar labelling initiative last year the moves come as the firms face criticism from consumer groups concerned at rising levels of obesity in us children major food manufacturers have recently been reformulating the content of some calorieheavy products

krafts new advertising policy which covers advertising on tv radio and in print publications is aimed at children between the ages of six and 11 it means commercials for some of its most famous snacks and cereals shown during early morning cartoon shows on tv will now be replaced by food and drink qualifying for krafts new sensible solution label but the firm said it would continue to advertise all its products in media seen by parents and all family audiences were working on ways to encourage both adults and children to eat wisely by selecting more nutritionally balanced diets said lance friedmann kraft senior vice president
